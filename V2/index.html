Overview

The 2008 Volkswagen R32 was a limited production vehicle based on the MK5 Golf platform. The R32 shares a lot of similarities with it's siblings when it comes to comfort and entertainment. At a glance this looks like a regular Golf or GTI but all the differences are under the hood and chassis. Production was limited for the R32 to just 5000 in the USA. Other countries also had limited production and seemingly unique to the USA is a 4-door version was never produced. 2008 is also the last year the VW Golf was produced in Germany for the US market.

Engine

Volkswagen Engineers managed to stuff the 3.2L VR6 performance engine under the hood. The engine is transverse mounted, meaning the "front" of the engine is at the right side of the vehicle. The 3.2L 24 valve VR6 engine produces 250 HP at 6,250 RPM and 236 lb. ft. of torque at 3,000 RPM. With some modifications the engine power can be increased to around 275HP, and many have added forced induction producing well above 400 HP.

Drivetrain

The power of the VR6 is delivered though a DSG DQ250 6-Speed Automatic Transmission coupled with a Haldex 4 AWD system. The DSG DQ250 makes quick work of gear changes with a shift time of about 0.2 seconds while the Haldex 4 (AKA: 4-Motion) intelligent all wheel drive system can provide up to 90% of the power to the rear wheels.

Styling and Badges

The R32 has styling cues that differ from its siblings such as the lower grille. The most notable is the R32 badge on the exterior and the steering wheel displays the R32 emblem with production serial number. A little known difference of the 2 door variants is the door trim of the Rabbit is textured black where the R32 and GTI received body color door trim which is only found on the 4 door Rabbit variant.
